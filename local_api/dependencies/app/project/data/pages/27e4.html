Genetic Drift

Genetic drift can happen when disease or a natural disaster kills a lot of individuals. The individuals that are left alive reproduce and only their alleles are passed onto offspring.

​​​​​​​For example, a forest fire kills more red beetles than green beetles. As the beetle population grows again there is now a higher frequency of green beetles allele in the population.

Natural Selection

In natural selection, a population evolves because of selective pressures that cause individuals with useful traits to survive and reproduce more often.

​​​​​​​A selective pressure could be causing FQ-resistant bacteria to survive and reproduce more often. This would increase the allele frequency for FQ resistance.
