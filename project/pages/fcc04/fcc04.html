This case is based on the real use of beehive fences in Africa. 
It was started by the Elephant and Bees Research Project.
Beehive fences naturally stop elephants from damaging farms.

Elephants are a which means they are very important. 
About 10,000-15,000 elephants are killed each year. 
That is why people had to find a way to stop elephants from eating crops
​​​​​​​without killing the elephants.

To lower the costs of the beehives, farmers will make fake hives too.
Fake hives look like the real beehives, so elephants are scared of fake hives.
This helps farmers protect more of their crops without spending more money.

The beehives also make honey that farmers can sell.
This helps farmers make more money from their farms.
