A hypothesis is what you think is a correct answer to a question. 


It also has the reasons why you think you are correct. 


A hypothesis can be tested to find out if it is correct.




For example, here is a question: “What would happen to plants if rabbits were added to a park?”


A hypothesis could be: “I think that if I added rabbits to a park then there would be less plants. This is because rabbits eat plants”


The hypothesis can be tested by adding rabbits to find out if it was correct.
